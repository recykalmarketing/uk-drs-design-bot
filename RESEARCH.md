# UK Deposit Return Scheme Research

Research review date: **13 July 2026**

## 1. What is the UK DRS?

A Deposit Return Scheme adds a refundable deposit to an eligible drinks container. The customer pays the deposit when buying the drink and receives it back after returning the empty container to an approved return point.

For the Exchange for Change scheme covering **England, Scotland and Northern Ireland**:

- Launch date: **1 October 2027**.
- Deposit: **20p per eligible container**.
- Materials: single-use **PET plastic, aluminium and steel** drinks containers.
- Size: **150ml to 3 litres**.
- Glass is not included in this three-nation scheme.
- Everyone in the drinks supply chain must pass the deposit through when selling eligible filled containers.
- Groceries retailers must generally operate a manual or automated return point unless exempt.

Official sources:

- GOV.UK retailer and producer responsibilities: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/deposit-return-scheme-drinks-producer-and-retailer-responsibilities
- Exchange for Change: https://exchangeforchange.co.uk/
- Exchange for Change FAQs: https://exchangeforchange.co.uk/faqs/

## 2. Why is it needed?

Defra states that around **25 billion** single-use drinks containers are bought each year across England, Scotland and Northern Ireland, and estimates that **6.5 billion** bottles and cans go to waste rather than being recycled each year.

The scheme is intended to address three linked problems:

1. **Litter:** Keep Britain Tidy states that drinks bottles and cans account for **55% of litter by volume**.
2. **Lost material value:** Mixed collection can contaminate material. A separate return stream can produce cleaner PET and metal suitable for higher-quality recycling.
3. **Virgin material demand:** Closed-loop collection can support bottle-to-bottle and can-to-can recycling, reducing dependence on newly extracted material.

Sources:

- Defra introduction: https://defraenvironment.blog.gov.uk/2025/01/31/introducing-the-deposit-return-scheme-for-drinks-containers/
- Keep Britain Tidy: https://www.keepbritaintidy.org/introducing-deposit-return-scheme

## 3. Expected environmental impact

The most defensible impact messages for public content are:

- higher return and recycling rates for eligible containers,
- less bottle and can litter in streets, parks, rivers and coastlines,
- cleaner PET and metal material streams,
- better potential for closed-loop recycling,
- reduced reliance on virgin materials,
- more consistent public recycling behaviour.

Exchange for Change is designing the scheme around a **90% collection rate within three years** of launch. This is a target, not a guaranteed outcome, and should be written as an ambition or target.

Avoid attributing all packaging-policy economic figures to DRS. The UK Government says the combined reforms of packaging EPR, simpler recycling and DRS could stimulate **£10 billion** in recycling investment over ten years and support up to **21,000 jobs in England**. These are combined-reform figures, not DRS-only figures.

Source:

- Packaging reforms: https://www.gov.uk/government/news/packuk-the-new-scheme-administrator-packaging-extended-producer-responsibility

## 4. Retailer responsibilities and exemptions

Retailers selling in-scope drinks must charge the 20p deposit. Supermarkets, grocery stores, convenience stores and newsagents selling scheme articles must generally register and operate a return point.

Return points can be:

- **Manual:** staff validate containers, issue refunds, bag, tag and store returns.
- **Automated:** an RVM scans and validates containers, issues the approved refund method, compacts material and stores it for collection.

Current exemptions and support announced by Exchange for Change:

- Urban stores under **100m²** are automatically exempt from hosting a return point.
- Urban retailers with **100m² to 199m²** sales area may apply for a size-based exemption.
- Rural retailers under **200m²** may apply for a size-based exemption.
- Other possible grounds include proximity to another return point, listed-building or heritage restrictions, site access, and insufficient utilities.
- Exempt retailers still charge the deposit when selling eligible drinks.

Source:

- https://exchangeforchange.co.uk/news/exchange-for-change-announces-drs-support-for-retailers/

## 5. Retailer grant and handling fee

Exchange for Change has announced:

- a **£60 million** grant fund,
- support for up to **10,000** qualifying small independent retailers,
- **£6,000 per eligible site**, paid in three annual instalments of £2,000 after RVM installation.

Return Handling Fee:

- Manual return point: **3p per container**.
- Automated return point tier 1: **5p per container**, up to 225,000 annual in-scope returns.
- Automated return point tier 2: **1.3p per container** above 225,000 annual returns.

The fee is intended to contribute to equipment, staffing, training, floor space and storage costs. It should not automatically be described as profit.

## 6. Official RVM requirements

The Exchange for Change specification says retailers should require evidence that an RVM meets the scheme's minimum requirements. Important areas include:

- integration with the product Article List,
- barcode reading,
- weight, metal and shape recognition,
- anti-fraud controls,
- PET and metal compaction,
- operational availability and connectivity,
- secure transaction reporting,
- encrypted data in transit and at rest,
- accessible consumer interfaces,
- approved deposit repayment methods,
- supplier certification before access to the scheme Article List.

Minimum recognition combines barcode, basic shape, weight and metal detection. Public-facing RVM claims should not imply UK accreditation until certification is complete.

Official specification:

- https://exchangeforchange.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/UK-DMO-RVM-Specification.pdf

## 7. Wales must be treated separately

Wales is progressing a separate DRS intended to start on **1 October 2027**. Its planned scope includes PET, aluminium, steel and glass containers from 150ml to 3 litres. Glass is planned to have a zero-pence deposit during a transition period ending **30 September 2031**.

The design bot therefore keeps Wales out of default Exchange for Change content and offers it only as a separate topic.

Official source:

- https://www.gov.wales/written-statement-deposit-return-scheme-drinks-containers-wales-regulations-2026
