# ReteaRn UK creative quality methodology

Version: RETEARN-UK-SOCIAL-2.1  
Copyright gate: RETEARN-UK-IP-1.0  
Approval threshold: 95/100

## Purpose

The score is a strict preflight for ReteaRn UK social creatives. It checks verified UK DRS information, the brief, global information-design principles, accessibility, ReteaRn UK identity, platform readiness and originality controls.

It is not a prediction of reach, engagement, leads or sales. The copyright component reduces risk but does not prove worldwide uniqueness or replace legal review.

## Score structure

### 1. UK DRS accuracy — 20 points

Checks official source attribution, geographical scope, supported facts and numbers, approved scheme language, Wales separation and avoidance of misleading claims. Factual or geographical failures prevent approval.

### 2. Brief and audience alignment — 15 points

Checks that the mandatory brief is materially reflected in the headline, body, CTA, caption, metrics and visual direction. The dashboard reports matched terms and brief implementation.

### 3. SEO and discoverability — 10 points

Checks focused UK DRS language, useful LinkedIn alt text and three to five relevant hashtags without keyword stuffing. Hashtags must come from the approved ReteaRn/generic whitelist.

### 4. Design hierarchy — 15 points

Checks one dominant message, a concise 3–12 word headline, a distinct supporting line, restrained body copy, no more than three metric cards and one action-led CTA.

### 5. Visual hierarchy — 15 points

Checks reading order, controlled text density, negative space, contrast protection, headline wrapping, exact top-left logo placement and the approved RVM workflow where a machine is required.

### 6. UK-friendly and accessible — 10 points

Checks British English, plain UK DRS terminology, correct geography, restrained acronyms and absence of US currency or wording.

### 7. ReteaRn UK and platform readiness — 5 points

Checks Poppins, supplied retearn.uk SVG, palette lock, supported aspect ratio, PNG readiness, source attribution and RVM-reference metadata.

### 8. Originality and copyright — 10 points

Checks:

- creator rights confirmation
- no copying, tracing or style-imitation instruction
- no third-party marks in copy or visual direction
- no long verbatim source-language overlap
- approved hashtags only
- original-composition prompt lock
- generation and asset provenance
- visual IP audit not blocked

A minimum 9/10 is required. Any critical originality issue blocks approval regardless of the overall score.

## Global design rules embedded

- one primary message per creative
- Gestalt grouping through proximity and similarity
- clear scale contrast between headline, support copy and metadata
- consistent alignment and spacing rhythm
- figure-ground separation for text readability
- limited cognitive load and progressive disclosure
- accessible contrast and text sizing
- platform-safe margins and readable line lengths
- visual evidence appropriate to the selected audience
- new composition rather than imitation of an existing campaign

## RVM accuracy and rights control

For RVM-led posts, the creative must record:

- `rvmRequired: true`
- `rvmReferenceUsed: true`
- the approved reference-bank version
- the approved rights-manifest asset IDs

The supplied RVM files are the only machine references. They are treated as user-declared owned or authorised. A generic, distorted, competitor-like or redesigned RVM cannot pass the automated gate. Human Design Manager approval remains mandatory.

## Interpretation

- 95–100 plus copyright pass: eligible for Brand Manager review
- 90–94: revise before submission
- below 90: substantial revision required
- copyright preflight blocked: cannot be submitted or published at any score

Both Brand Manager and Design Manager rights attestations are required before LinkedIn publishing.
