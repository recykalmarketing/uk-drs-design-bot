# UK RVM Competitor Research

Research review date: **13 July 2026**

This landscape is for internal positioning and content planning. Public competitor-comparison posts should receive legal and sales approval before publication.

## Market context

Exchange for Change plans to publish or maintain accredited supplier guidance. Retail buyers will evaluate more than the machine purchase price. The official specification makes compliance, data integration, recognition accuracy, accessibility, certification and transaction security central to procurement.

The design bot therefore includes neutral public topics such as **“What retailers should ask an RVM supplier”** rather than unsupported comparative advertising.

## TOMRA

Official positioning observed:

- extensive global RVM experience and installed base,
- UK-specific range spanning small stores to large supermarkets,
- compact, standalone and multi-feed options,
- digital APIs and operational tools,
- strong education and retailer-readiness content.

Examples on its UK site include small-format machines, medium-store standalone machines and high-volume multi-feed systems.

Sources:

- https://www.tomra.com/en-gb/reverse-vending/our-offering/reverse-vending-machines
- https://www.tomra.com/reverse-vending/media-center/feature-articles/what-retailers-need-to-know-when-buying-reverse-vending-machine

Implication for ReteaRn:

- Do not compete only on “AI”.
- Make the commercial and operating model easy to understand.
- Lead with retailer fit: footprint, expected volume, uptime, service, capex model and customer experience.

## Envipco

- customised solutions from small to large enterprises,
- a portfolio including compact machines for limited spaces and larger multi-material solutions,
- active UK staffing and rollout preparation.

Sources:

## StrongPoint and other integrators

Retail technology and service partners can influence rollout, installation and field support even when they are not the RVM manufacturer. The UK sales approach should map:

- retailer procurement teams,
- facilities and store-development teams,
- IT and security teams,
- sustainability and compliance owners,
- installation and maintenance partners.

## Recommended ReteaRn positioning pillars

Based on the supplied product brochure, the strongest potential pillars are:

1. **Managed operating model:** zero-upfront-capex proposition, ownership and maintenance handled by ReteaRn.
2. **Compact deployment:** 10.9 sq ft footprint claim, subject to confirmation in UK installation conditions.
3. **Operational automation:** unattended operation, monitoring and remote updates.
4. **Retailer economics:** return handling fee plus lower operational workload, without describing the fee as guaranteed profit.
5. **Data-led service:** operational dashboard, service visibility and compliant transaction reporting.
6. **Readiness support:** site survey, volume forecast, exemption/grant guidance and staff/customer launch kit.

## Claims that need proof before a UK campaign

- UK DMO or Exchange for Change certification.
- Uptime percentage.
- Recognition speed or accuracy under the final UK Article List.
- Service response time in each UK region.
- Cost savings against named competitors.
- “Best”, “fastest”, “smallest” or similar superiority claims.
- Guaranteed annual income.
