# UK DRS visual research applied to the bot

The user-provided Google image and web-search URLs were treated as research prompts, not as a downloadable image bank. Third-party retailer and competitor images are not copied into the product.

## Visual patterns implemented

- A clear circular journey: buy, pay deposit, return, reclaim, recycle.
- Recognisable PET bottle and metal-can silhouettes.
- One clear return action rather than complicated multi-scene storytelling.
- Realistic British supermarket, convenience-store and forecourt contexts.
- Close, accessible RVM interaction with a visible inlet.
- Retailer decision trees, timelines, included-versus-excluded comparisons and material loops.
- Strong negative space and no more than three data cards.
- Separate treatment for Wales where the scheme scope differs.
- No Irish Re-turn branding, Irish deposit values or competitor machine branding presented as UK scheme facts.

## Official research sources locked into the product

- GOV.UK retailer and producer responsibilities
- Exchange for Change official pages, retailer guidance and FAQs
- England and Northern Ireland regulations on legislation.gov.uk
- Defra environmental explainer
- Keep Britain Tidy impact evidence where used by an approved topic

## RVM rule

Whenever a reverse vending machine is required, the approved ReteaRn UK reference bank remains the single source of truth. The bot must not invent a generic machine or copy a competitor machine seen in search results.
